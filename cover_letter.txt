Dear Editor,
We are pleased to submit our original research article entitled 
"Machine Learning Prediction of Obesity Risk in Hispanic and 
Non-Hispanic Black Children: A Comparative Analysis Using 
NHANES 2021-2023 Data" for consideration for publication in 
Pediatric Obesity.

BACKGROUND AND IMPORTANCE:
Childhood obesity disproportionately affects Hispanic and 
Non-Hispanic Black children in the United States, yet machine 
learning approaches specifically targeting these underserved 
populations remain limited. Addressing this gap is critical 
given the well-documented racial and ethnic disparities in 
pediatric obesity prevalence and the urgent need for targeted 
prevention strategies in these communities.

WHAT WE DID:
Using the most recent nationally representative NHANES 
2021-2023 data, we developed and compared two machine learning 
models — Random Forest and XGBoost — specifically focused on 
Hispanic and Non-Hispanic Black children aged 2-19 years. 
Our final analytic sample comprised 1,102 participants with 
19 clinical, socioeconomic, dietary, and physical activity 
variables.

KEY FINDINGS:
Our XGBoost model achieved an overall accuracy of 92.8% with 
near-perfect obesity detection (precision=0.97, recall=0.98), 
representing a meaningful improvement over previously published 
models. Feature importance analysis revealed that socioeconomic 
factors and physical activity measures — particularly 
income-to-poverty ratio and PE class attendance — were stronger 
predictors of obesity than race alone, with important 
implications for intervention design in these communities.

WHY THIS JOURNAL:
We believe this manuscript is ideally suited for Pediatric 
Obesity as it directly addresses the journal's focus on 
research into obesity during childhood and adolescence, 
specifically covering epidemiology, behavior, physical 
activity, and health disparities. Our findings provide 
actionable clinical insights for pediatricians and public 
health practitioners working with Hispanic and Black 
pediatric populations.

ORIGINALITY:
This study is original, has not been published previously, 
and is not under consideration for publication elsewhere. 
All data and code are publicly available at 
https://github.com/TatiBen/pediatric-obesity-ml ensuring
