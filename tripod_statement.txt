
REPORTING GUIDELINE COMPLIANCE

This study was reported in accordance with the TRIPOD+AI statement 
(Transparent Reporting of a multivariable prediction model for 
Individual Prognosis Or Diagnosis — Artificial Intelligence), 
published in BMJ 2024. The TRIPOD+AI checklist was completed and 
is available as supplementary material.

Key TRIPOD+AI items addressed in this study:
1. TITLE: Clearly identifies study as machine learning prediction model
2. ABSTRACT: Structured abstract with background, methods, results, 
   conclusions
3. INTRODUCTION: Objectives and clinical context clearly stated
4. DATA SOURCE: NHANES 2021-2023 publicly available dataset
5. PARTICIPANTS: Inclusion/exclusion criteria clearly defined
6. OUTCOME: Primary outcome (BMDBMIC) clearly defined with CDC criteria
7. PREDICTORS: All 17 predictor variables listed and described
8. SAMPLE SIZE: Training (n=881) and test (n=221) sets reported
9. MISSING DATA: Median imputation for continuous variables, 
   mode imputation for categorical variables
10. STATISTICAL METHODS: Random Forest and XGBoost algorithms 
    with all hyperparameters reported
11. MODEL PERFORMANCE: Accuracy, precision, recall, F1-score 
    reported for all BMI categories
12. FEATURE IMPORTANCE: Variable importance analysis conducted 
    and reported
13. LIMITATIONS: Clearly discussed including cross-sectional design,
    small underweight sample, recall bias
14. REPRODUCIBILITY: All code publicly available at 
    https://github.com/TatiBen/pediatric-obesity-ml
15. CONFLICTS OF INTEREST: None declared
16. DATA AVAILABILITY: NHANES data freely available from CDC
